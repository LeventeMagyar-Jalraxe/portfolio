Through traditional drawing and digital illumination, I explore the relationship between nature, symbolism and transformation. My work seeks moments where light becomes more than a visual effect—it becomes meaning.

Guardians of Light

Guardians of Light presents critically endangered species through luminous portraits. Each artwork highlights the beauty and fragility of Earth's most threatened wildlife.

Pygmy Three-Toed Sloth (Bradypus pygmaeus)

2025 · Colored pencil and digital lightwork · 30 × 40 cm

The first featured species: This charming, cat-sized little creature is one of the rarest mammals on Earth — only about 100 individuals remain, living exclusively on a tiny island.

Tapanuli Orangutan (Pongo tapanuliensis)

2026 · Colored pencil and digital lightwork · 30 × 40 cm

The rarest great ape species on Earth, with fewer than 800 individuals remaining in the wild.

Andean mountain cat (Leopardus jacobita)

2026 · Colored pencil and digital lightwork · 30 × 40 cm

The Andean mountain cat is one of the rarest and least known wild cat species in the world. It is estimated that fewer than 1,500 individuals remain in the wild, living in isolated populations across the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

Proposed Partner: Andean Cat Alliance

Chinese Pangolin (Manis pentadactyla)

2026 · Colored pencil and digital lightwork · 30 × 40 cm

Meet the next Guardian of Light: the Chinese pangolin (Manis pentadactyla). The only mammal wrapped in hard, protective scales — once nature's perfect armor. Today, it can't protect them from humans. Poaching and illegal trade make them one of the most endangered and trafficked mammals on Earth.

Proposed Partner: Save Pangolins

Blue-throated Macaw (Ara glaucogularis)

2026 · Colored pencil and digital lightwork · 30 × 40 cm

The Blue-throated Macaw is one of the rarest parrot species in the world, pushed to the brink of extinction by habitat loss and the exotic pet trade.

Proposed Partner: Asociación Armonía
